Gender-Based Violence in South Africa

Critical reading activity based on the article "Sweden on the road: Empowering South Africa's next generation"

Article Summary

The article discusses a collaborative initiative between Sweden and South Africa to empower young leaders in the fight against Gender-Based Violence (GBV). The "Sweden on the Road" Women's Month edition brought together students, youth activists, academics, policymakers, and civil society organisations to address GBV challenges and develop youth-centred solutions.

Analytical Questions

1. What types of evidence does the author use to support the claim that youth leadership is central to addressing gender-based violence? Consider whether the evidence is statistical, anecdotal, institutional (e.g. referencing the National Strategic Plan), or testimonial (e.g., quotes from officials). How does this shape the credibility of the argument?

2. How does the author construct the identities of South African youth and Swedish partners, and what persuasive purpose does this serve? Look at how young people are described (e.g., "courage, energy, creativity") and how Sweden is positioned as a supportive ally. What assumptions or values are embedded in these portrayals?

3. In what ways does the article appeal to cultural values or shared histories between South Africa and Sweden? Identify references to "historic partnership," "shared commitment," or "global advocacy." How do these cultural and diplomatic appeals reinforce the message and invite reader alignment?

4. Which emotional appeals are used to engage the reader, and how do they function within the broader argument? Pay attention to language that evokes hope, urgency, or pride‚Äîespecially in phrases like "empowering young leaders" or "solutions to South Africa's toughest challenges." How do these affect the reader's response?

5. How does the article position the reader in relation to the issue‚Äîare they being invited to act, reflect, or admire?

4 Key Tips for Integrating Your Analysis

  • Connect the evidence types to their effectiveness in supporting the argument's credibility
  • Analyse how the portrayal of different groups serves the argument's persuasive purpose
  • Consider how cultural and diplomatic appeals create alignment with the reader
  • Reflect on how emotional appeals position the reader to respond to the issue

Reflection Questions

1. How does the article's emphasis on youth leadership resonate with your own role as a student or emerging professional? In what ways do you see yourself contributing to social change, particularly around gender-based violence?

2. What connections can you draw between the article's themes and your academic discipline or field of study? How might your discipline help address or understand the crisis of GBV in South Africa?

3. Reflect on your identity as a young African. How does the article's portrayal of youth empowerment align or clash with your lived experience? Consider how cultural, social, or institutional factors shape your perspective.
